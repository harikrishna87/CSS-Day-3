Tea Making:

  1. Boil Water: Heat water in a kettle or pot until it reaches a rolling boil.

  2. Add Tea Leaves: Add tea leaves or tea bags to the boiling water and let it steep for 3-5 minutes.
  3. Add Sugar and Spices: Optional - Add sugar, spices like cardamom, or ginger for extra flavor.
  4. Pour Milk: Add milk to the tea, bring it to a gentle boil again, and simmer for a minute.
  5. Strain and Serve: Strain the tea into cups and serve hot.

Dosa Making:

  1. Prepare Batter: Soak rice and urad dal separately, grind them into a smooth batter, and ferment overnight.

  2. Heat the Pan: Heat a non-stick pan or tawa, and lightly grease it with oil.
  3. Spread the Batter: Pour a ladle of batter onto the pan and spread it thinly in a circular motion.
  4. Cook the Dosa: Drizzle a little oil around the edges, cook until the dosa is crispy and golden.
  5. Serve Hot: Fold the dosa, remove from the pan, and serve with chutney and sambar.

Upma Making:

  1. Roast Rava: Dry roast rava (semolina) in a pan until it turns light golden and aromatic.

  2. Prepare Tempering: Heat oil, add mustard seeds, urad dal, curry leaves, green chilies, and chopped onions.
  3. Add Water: Pour water into the pan, bring it to a boil, and add salt to taste.
  4. Cook Rava: Gradually add the roasted rava to the boiling water while stirring continuously.
  5. Simmer and Serve: Cook on low heat until the rava absorbs the water, then garnish with coriander and serve hot.

Biryani Making:

  1. Marinate the Meat: Marinate chicken or meat with yogurt, spices, and herbs for at least 1-2 hours.

  2. Cook Rice: Partially cook basmati rice in boiling water with whole spices, then drain.
  3. Layering: In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer the marinated meat and partially cooked rice alternately.
  4. Dum Cooking: Seal the pot with a lid, cook on low heat (dum) for 30-40 minutes until the flavors meld.
  5. Serve with Raita: Fluff up the biryani, garnish with fried onions, and serve with raita or salan.
